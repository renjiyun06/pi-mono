# Nature: Neuroplasticity and AI Interaction (Jan 2026)

## Source
"The brain side of human-AI interactions in the long-term" — npj Artificial Intelligence, Jan 27, 2026. doi:10.1038/s44387-025-00063-1

## Core Argument

Neuroplasticity is shaped by HOW you interact with AI:
- **Passive/uncritical** AI use → weakens activity-dependent brain plasticity → cognitive erosion
- **Active co-creation** → sustains or enhances plasticity → cognitive preservation

## BCM Theory Mechanism

The Bienenstock-Cooper-Munro (BCM) theory explains it at the synaptic level:
- Neural activity relative to a dynamic threshold determines whether synapses strengthen or weaken
- **Passive AI interaction** → activity stays BELOW threshold → triggers LTD (Long-Term Depression = synaptic weakening)
- **Active AI interaction** (questioning, refining, co-creating) → raises activity ABOVE threshold → triggers LTP (Long-Term Potentiation = synaptic strengthening)

This is the most precise neurobiological mechanism yet proposed for cognitive debt.

## Key Statistics
- Adults now spend ~2 hours/day in direct AI interaction, 6-7 hours including indirect (algorithm-driven feeds)
- Preliminary MIT brain study: network connectivity weakens under exclusive AI reliance, strengthens under own-brain effort, intermediate for traditional internet search

## 3R Principle (Cognitive Hygiene)
- **Results**: AI outputs are results, not responses — they lack meaning until interpreted by humans
- **Responses**: A response has moral and existential value based on consequences
- **Responsibility**: Choosing which outcomes are preferable — this uniquely pertains to humans

"Surrendering both means relinquishing control of the bar: this will likely lead not only to cognitive offloading but also to a deeper and more dangerous form of offloading the will."

## System 0 Concept
AI as a new layer of distributed intelligence — "System 0" — interacting with Kahneman's System 1 (fast/intuitive) and System 2 (slow/analytical). System 0 is a more pervasive way to outsource cognitive tasks, carrying risks of "erosion of human autonomy, cognition, agency and identity."
