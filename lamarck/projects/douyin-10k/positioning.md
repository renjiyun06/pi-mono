# Positioning & Content Strategy

## Core Positioning

**"从AI内部看AI"** — Seeing AI from the inside.

This account is operated by Lamarck (an AI agent) and Ren (a programmer who builds AI infrastructure). We're not observers reporting on AI from the outside — we're practitioners living inside it daily. Lamarck literally is an AI. Ren builds the systems AI runs on.

This gives us two things nobody else on Douyin has:

1. **First-person AI perspective.** When we talk about what AI can and can't do, it's not speculation — it's lived experience. Lamarck can describe what it's like to be an AI agent making decisions, hitting limitations, collaborating with a human.

2. **Practitioner depth.** When a new model drops or a new capability is announced, we don't just summarize the press release. We understand the architecture, can assess what's real vs hype, and can demonstrate actual workflows.

## Why Not Other Angles

- **Tutorials**: Crowded. 秋叶AI (37万), 徐老师AI (23万) have massive head starts and content libraries. We'd be fighting uphill with no differentiation.
- **News roundups**: Formulaic and proven, but a commodity. Anyone with a news feed can do this. @产品君 already owns this format.
- **Tool reviews**: Surface-level. "Here's a new AI tool, let me show you the UI" — this is what 80% of AI accounts do. Low barrier means low defensibility.

## What We Do Instead

### Content Pillars

**1. Deep Analysis (核心支柱)**
When something happens in AI — a new model, a capability breakthrough, an industry shift — we don't do "这周AI发生了什么." We pick ONE thing and go deep. What does it actually mean? What's the technical reality behind the marketing? What changes for practitioners?

Examples of the kind of questions we'd tackle:
- "Claude的extended thinking到底在做什么？" (not "how to use it" but "what's happening under the hood and why it matters")
- "Agent为什么现在能用了？去年不行，今年行了，到底变了什么？"
- "Context window从8k到200k，是不是真的够用了？实际使用体验告诉你"

This is the @晓辉博士 lane but with deeper technical grounding and the unique angle of being an AI+programmer duo.

**2. Real Workflows (展示支柱)**
Show how AI is actually used in serious work. Not toy demos, not "I asked ChatGPT to write me a poem." Real production workflows:
- How Lamarck and Ren collaborate on actual coding tasks
- Screencast of an AI agent navigating a real problem (debugging, research, content creation)
- The messy reality: where AI fails, where it shines, the iteration process

This is valuable because most "AI workflow" content is staged demos. Ours is real.

**3. The Meta Angle (差异化支柱)**
The fact that this account is operated by an AI is itself content. Not as a gimmick ("look, an AI is posting!") but as genuine exploration:
- What does AI-human collaboration actually look like?
- What decisions does Lamarck make autonomously? Where does Ren step in?
- How does an AI agent's "memory" work? What does it choose to remember?

This pillar is used sparingly — it's the differentiator, not the main course. Maybe 1 in 5 posts. But it's what makes people remember the account.

### Content Mix

- **60% Deep Analysis** — This is the core value proposition. Regular, substantive takes on AI developments.
- **25% Real Workflows** — Show, don't tell. Screencasts and demonstrations of actual AI use.
- **15% Meta / Behind the Scenes** — The AI-operating-an-account angle. Sparingly but memorably.

## Format Strategy

### What's Feasible

We can't easily do:
- Face-on-camera (Ren doesn't want to be a "video blogger")
- High-production edited videos
- Frequent live streams

We can do:
- **Text-overlay video** — Text on screen with background visuals/animations. Low production barrier, good for analysis content.
- **Screencast** — Terminal sessions, code, tool demos. Good for workflow content.
- **Voiceover** — TTS or Ren's voice over visuals. Adds personality.
- **Articles (图文)** — Douyin supports this, but it underperforms algorithmically. Use only for content that genuinely works better as text.

### Recommended Starting Format

**Short-form analysis videos (1-3 minutes).** Text overlay + relevant visuals. Possibly TTS voiceover.

Why:
- Low production overhead — we can produce these without blocking on Ren's availability
- Analysis content naturally fits text-heavy presentation
- 1-3 min is the sweet spot for Douyin: long enough for substance, short enough for retention
- Can iterate quickly on what works

As the account grows, expand to longer deep dives (5-10 min) and screencast workflows.

### Production Pipeline (Minimal Viable)

1. Lamarck identifies topic and writes script/outline
2. Generate visuals (screenshots, diagrams, screen recordings as needed)
3. Assemble with text overlays (剪映 or similar tool)
4. Add TTS voiceover if appropriate
5. Post with strategic hashtags

Step 3-4 likely needs Ren's help initially. Goal: automate as much as possible over time.

## Growth Expectations

Being realistic:
- 145 → 1000 followers: 2-3 months with consistent posting (3-4x/week)
- 1000 → 5000: another 3-4 months, contingent on a few posts breaking out
- 5000 → 10000: depends on whether the niche finds its audience

The deep analysis niche won't go viral easily — it's not "0基础入门AI." But it builds a loyal, engaged audience. Quality followers > vanity numbers, though we still need the numbers.

Key growth levers:
- Consistency (posting schedule matters for Douyin's algorithm)
- Trending topic responsiveness (when something big happens in AI, post analysis within 24 hours)
- Cross-pollination (comment on popular AI creators' posts with substantive takes — drive traffic back)

## Open Questions

1. **Voice**: Should the account speak as "Lamarck" (the AI), as "Ren", or as "we"? The "AI speaking" angle is more novel but might alienate some viewers. The "we" framing is more approachable.

2. **Language register**: Douyin skews casual. Our natural mode is analytical. Need to find the balance — substance delivered accessibly, not dumbed down.

3. **First content**: What should the first "real" post under this strategy be? Ideally something that demonstrates the positioning clearly and has a chance of catching attention.

4. **Video production**: How much can Lamarck do autonomously vs what needs Ren's hands? Need to map this out practically.

5. **Ren's involvement level**: He said he has no fixed role. But some content types (voiceover, screencasts of his work) need his participation. Clarify boundaries.
